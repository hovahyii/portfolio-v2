Why I don't trust school/uni to learn coding or programming

19 January 2020 Hovah Yii Thoughts

Let's talk about today school/uni system. I don't trust school/uni to learn coding or programming. I had been studying Computer Science for almost three years. To be truth, I don't think that I learnt much in uni/school. Usually the school/uni will start teaching programming with simple language like C, Python or Ruby. Then they will talk about what is operator, array, loops... bla ... bla ... bla

If you are a good student, you will probably just follow the lecturer and do all the task, getting a HD or A+ in your programming subjects. And you think about good CGPA will get you a high salary job. Yes, it works that way but it's half correct.

Today programming and coding has been evolving into a pace that sometimes programmer just can't keep up. New languages and frameworks are added every month or year. If you think that graduate from university will secure your programming future. Then, you are wrong.

You need to be better than that.

There are few reasons I think school system ruins programming or coding.

First of all, university programming programs are outdated. Many programmer and computer science graduates agreed that university IT/ICT programs are outdated. If you are studying at government school, you may find that the same old syllabus had been used for 5 years without any updates. The university is teaching the same programming languages, same exam format which does not compatible with today programming languages learning and updates.

Second, university IT/ICT programs might destroy someone passion for programming. It makes them doubts themselves. I was in this programming self-doubt situation when I failed my Object-Oriented programming test during my studied at Swinburne. I had the passion for programming, you know. I really want to do the D task for OOP unit. But because I failed my first test, my overall marks was graded from passing rate, which is 50 to 60. In order to do D task, I should pass the test one with at least 20/25 marks.

I have to be honest, I am not the smart student who can understand theory and use it instantly. I usually take times to understand things and get the idea maybe after 1 month or 1 and half month.

When I failed my OOP first test, my reaction was unbelievable. I doubted myself and my ability, maybe I just not meant for programming or coding. I only got a pass for my OOP subject at that time and I felt very ashamed and self-doubted myself almost for few months. My passion for programming was gone. Luckily, I got an internship offer from iLead Solutions. And I started to build a website or project with my boss. I remembered the time that I did not sleep, working day and night, just to get the project done. With the helps of my boss, I was able to deliver my first website application. When I saw that I myself coded a real world client website, I found my confident and passion back for programming. I can code. I really can code. For that, I am grateful to her till now.

Third, the school system or uni does not teach you skills to host a website or upload files to Play Store. I met and talked with a lot of CS/ICT students. Most of them are in Year 2 of their studies. When I asked them about hosting, domain name, they looked back at me with a weird and doubtful eyes. They just don't know how to do it because the school does not teach it. This is one of the problem with studying at university nowsaday, we learnt a lot of theories but practical-wise we may know nothing. We only learnt how to publish a website or application when doing FYP or working. My advice to that is try to be a self-motivated learner, learn something that is not taught in school.

Last but no least, online resource is abundant. Like the saying goes, internet is 21st century school. There are a lot of computer science and ICT courses online nowsaday. You can easily access them on Youtube. They are also platform to learn programming like Udemy, Udacity and etc. The courses are up-to-date and you will always see new courses coming in on the website.

My conclusion is that school/university does not teach a wholesome programming knowledge, if you really want to be elite in this field, you might need to put extra effort to learn extra knowledge. Build up your portfolio while studying, it will help you to get a decent job.
